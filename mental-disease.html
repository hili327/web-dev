Mental Diseases Information

Depression

Symptoms: Persistent sadness, loss of interest or pleasure in activities, changes in appetite or weight, sleep disturbances, fatigue, feelings of worthlessness or guilt.

Causes: Complex interaction of genetic, biological, environmental, and psychological factors.

Treatment: Therapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy, psychotherapy), medications (antidepressants).

Alzheimer's Disease

Symptoms: Memory loss, confusion, difficulty with language, changes in mood and behavior.

Causes: Brain cell death and tissue loss.

Treatment: Medications, management of symptoms, support for caregivers.

Anxiety Disorders

Symptoms: Excessive worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbances.

Causes: Genetic and environmental factors, brain chemistry, life events.

Treatment: Therapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy, exposure therapy), medications (antidepressants, anti-anxiety medications).

Bipolar Disorder

Symptoms: Extreme mood swings, including emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression).

Causes: Genetics, neurotransmitter imbalances, environmental factors.

Treatment: Medications (mood stabilizers, antipsychotics), psychotherapy.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Symptoms: Recurring, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions).

Causes: Genetics, brain structure and function, environmental factors.

Treatment: Therapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy), medications (SSRIs).

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

Symptoms: Flashbacks, nightmares, severe anxiety, uncontrollable thoughts about the event.

Causes: Exposure to traumatic events.

Treatment: Therapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy), medications (antidepressants).

Schizophrenia

Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking, impaired functioning.

Causes: Genetics, brain chemistry, environment.

Treatment: Medications (antipsychotics), therapy, social support.

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

Symptoms: Inattention, hyperactivity, impulsivity.

Causes: Genetics, brain function and structure.

Treatment: Medications (stimulants, non-stimulants), therapy.

Eating Disorders

Symptoms: Severe disturbances in eating behavior, preoccupation with food, body weight, and shape.

Causes: Genetics, psychological factors, cultural and societal influences.

Treatment: Therapy (cognitive-behavioral therapy), nutritional counseling, medications.

Dissociative Disorders

Symptoms: Disruptions in identity, memory, or consciousness, such as dissociative identity disorder (DID) or dissociative amnesia.

Causes: Trauma, stress, dissociation as a coping mechanism.

Treatment: Therapy (psychotherapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy), medications for symptoms.

Paranoia

Symptoms: Persistent and irrational mistrust or suspicion of others, often leading to distress or impaired functioning.

Causes: May include genetic predisposition, stress, or other mental health disorders.

Treatment: Therapy (psychotherapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy), medications for related symptoms.

Psychosis

Symptoms: Loss of contact with reality, including hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking.

Causes: Various mental health disorders, substance use, medical conditions.

Treatment: Medications (antipsychotics), therapy (psychotherapy, cognitive-behavioral therapy), treatment of underlying conditions.
